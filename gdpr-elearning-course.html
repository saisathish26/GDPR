What is the General Data Protection Regulation?

The GDPR is a landmark EU regulation governing how organisations collect, process, and protect the personal data of individuals within the EU and EEA.

It came into force on 25 May 2018, replacing the outdated 1995 Data Protection Directive and setting a global gold standard for data privacy.

Non-compliance can result in fines of up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover — whichever is higher.

95

1995 — EU Data Protection Directive

Original framework providing basic data protection guidance, inconsistently applied across member states.

12

2012 — European Commission Proposal

Drafted comprehensive reform in response to the digital revolution, rise of social media and cloud computing.

16

2016 — GDPR Officially Adopted

After four years of negotiation, the European Parliament adopted the GDPR on 14 April 2016, with a 2-year preparation period.

18

2018 — GDPR Comes Into Force

Became fully enforceable on 25 May 2018. Within one year, over €56 million in fines had been issued across the EU.

🏢

EU-based Organisations

Any organisation in the EU that processes personal data, regardless of where processing occurs.

🌍

Non-EU Organisations

Organisations outside the EU offering goods/services to, or monitoring behaviour of, EU residents.

👤

Data Controllers

Entities determining the purposes and means of processing personal data. They bear primary GDPR responsibility.

⚙️

Data Processors

Third parties processing data on behalf of a controller, such as cloud providers or payroll services.

🙋

Data Subjects

Any identifiable natural person whose personal data is being processed. They hold the rights GDPR protects.

🔒

What is "Personal Data"?

Any information relating to an identifiable person: names, emails, IP addresses, location data, health records.

The 7 GDPR Principles

03
01
Article 5(1)(a)

Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency

Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently. Organisations must have a valid legal basis and clearly inform data subjects how their data is used.

Privacy noticesConsent formsLegal basis registerCookie policies
⚖️
Legal basis must be documented before any processing begins
02
Article 5(1)(b)

Purpose Limitation

Data must be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes. It must not be processed in a manner incompatible with those original purposes without fresh consent.

Purpose documentationData inventoryProcessing agreements
🎯
Collect data for one purpose — don't repurpose without consent
03
Article 5(1)(c)

Data Minimisation

Only data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the intended purpose should be collected. Avoid collecting data "just in case" it might be useful later.

Form field auditsData mappingRetention policies
✂️
Collect only what you truly need — nothing more
04
Article 5(1)(d)

Accuracy

Personal data must be accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date. Every reasonable step must be taken to ensure inaccurate data is erased or corrected without delay.

Regular data auditsUpdate mechanismsData quality checks
✅
Maintain accurate records and correct errors promptly
05
Article 5(1)(e)

Storage Limitation

Data must not be kept in identifiable form longer than necessary. Define and enforce clear data retention schedules for all categories of personal data.

Retention schedulesAutomated deletionAnonymisation
🗑️
Don't hold data longer than you need it
06
Article 5(1)(f)

Integrity & Confidentiality

Personal data must be processed with appropriate security, including protection against unauthorised processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

EncryptionAccess controlsSecurity testingStaff training
🔐
Protect data with technical and organisational measures
07
Article 5(2)

Accountability

The controller is responsible for, and must demonstrate compliance with, all GDPR principles. This means maintaining records, conducting DPIAs, and appointing a DPO where required.

ROPA recordsDPIAsDPO appointmentPolicy documentation
📋
Document everything — you must prove compliance

8 Rights of Data Subjects

Under GDPR, every individual has these powerful rights over their personal data

Request deletion of personal data — the "right to be forgotten".

Block processing while a dispute about data accuracy is resolved.

📦

🚫

Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

Art. 22
🤖

Rights re: Automated Decisions

Not be subject to solely automated decisions that significantly affect you.

Data Breach Response

05

Response Steps

1

Detect & Identify

Identify that a breach has occurred, assess scope and nature, log the incident immediately.

2

Contain the Breach

Take immediate action to stop the breach — disconnect systems, revoke access.

3

Assess the Risk

Evaluate likelihood and severity of impact on data subjects. Determine if notification is required.

4

Notify the ICO / DPA

Report to the supervisory authority within 72 hours if the breach poses a risk.

5

Notify Affected Individuals

If high risk, communicate the breach to affected data subjects without undue delay.

6

Review & Improve

Conduct a post-incident review. Update security measures and document lessons learned.

72h

Under GDPR Article 33, notify your supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach that poses a risk to data subjects' rights and freedoms.

Document the breach in your incident log
Record what categories of data were affected
Note the number of individuals affected
Describe likely consequences of the breach
List all measures taken to address it

Key Takeaways

⚖️GDPR applies to any organisation processing EU residents' data, regardless of where the organisation is based.
🎯Always establish and document a lawful basis before processing personal data — consent is just one of six options.
🔐Security is not optional. Implement encryption, access controls, and regular testing to protect personal data.
⏰Breach notification to the supervisory authority must happen within 72 hours if there's a risk to individuals.
👤Data subjects have 8 powerful rights. Your organisation must respond to requests within 30 days.
📋Accountability means documenting everything — ROPA, DPIAs, training records, and consent logs.
